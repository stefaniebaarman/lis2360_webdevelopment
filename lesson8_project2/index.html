What is Virtual Reality?

Virtual reality is the term used to describe a three-dimensional, computer generated environment which can be explored and interacted with by a person.

When Was Virtual Reality Invented?

Virtual reality technically began in the 1950’s but early elements of it can be traced back to the 1860’s and long before the development of digital technology.

The Sensorama

In the mid 1950s cinematographer Morton Heilig developed the Sensorama which was an arcade-style theatre cabinet that would stimulate all the senses, not just sight and sound.

Myron Krueger

He worked on making virtual reality devices portable and affordable.

Morton Helig

He created The Sensorama which featured stereo speakers, a stereoscopic 3D display, fans, smell generators and a vibrating chair to enhance cinema experience.

Ivan Sutherland

He created the first VR head mounted display that was connected to a computer and not a camera.

George Harrison

He worked with Ivan Sutherland to improve modern technology, such as laser printers and personal computers.

Virtual Reality In Education!

Teachers are using VR headsets to allow students to take a field trip, without leaving the classroom. Students can now travel to exotic countries, museums, and historic landmakrs using softwares such as Google Earth VR and Google Expeditions.

Virtual Reality In Shopping!

A user wears a VR headset and sensory gloves to get an in-store experience at home.

Virtual Reality In Military Training!

Virtual reality is used by the military for training purposes. It teaches them how to properly react in given situations, without the risk of death or injury.
